Wobbly Beginnings

When I was a kid, my parents brought home a Commodore 64, the first computer my family ever owned. Figuring out how to do anything with the C64 took us a long time, because nothing about that beige machine was intuitive.  We'd plod through manuals and type out the commands, eventually managing to load the simple programs and video games that friends had copied onto floppy disks.  As we grew more comfortable, we tried to write out simple, text-base adventure games, full of dragons and magic swords.  Yes, making games and figuring out how to get our computer to spit out the results that we wanted was strangely fun.

Today's computers hide most of the coding behind wonderfully designed user interfaces.  I'm not nostalgic for the C64, but I do miss the sense of discovery that I used to have.  I want to understand a lot more about how computers and programs work.  With that in mind, I decided to start this blog.

In this blog, I want to share my experience of learning how to program.  Along with my coding mishaps and (hopefully) achievements, I hope to post anything that I find about creating code, interesting websites, and the coding communities that I visit.

Where to Start?

Luckily for anyone trying to learn about computers and programming, there are so many free resources available online. Gone are the days of flipping through dry, technical manuals.

I'm going to spend the first few weeks of my exploration working through the exercises offered in these popular computer programming web sites:

  • http://www.codecademy.com/

  • http://www.codeavengers.com/

  • http://www.khanacademy.org/computing/cs

At this early stage, I'm still trying to figure out what languages are the most useful/accessible/widely used.  But, I do know that the web is built on HTML and JavaScript, so these seem like good places to start.

If anyone knows of other sites like these or has recommendations, please add a comment below and I'll check them out.
